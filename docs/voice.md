# Voice & Tone Guide

Read before writing any headlines, body copy, or bio content on this site.

---

## Who This Site Is For

Brands, marketing managers, and founders looking to hire a digital marketer for social media, email, or full channel strategy.

---

## Tone

- **Direct.** No fluff. Say what you mean in fewer words.
- **Confident, not arrogant.** Let results do the talking.
- **Specific.** "10 years, 50+ brands, Klaviyo certified" beats "extensive experience."
- **First-person.** "I build..." not "We help brands..."
- **Casual but professional.** Conversational sentences. No corporate jargon.

---

## What to Avoid

- Overused phrases: "data-driven", "holistic approach", "leverage", "synergy", "passionate"
- Passive voice
- Vague claims without numbers to back them up
- Long paragraphs — keep body copy under 3 sentences per block
- "Learn more" as CTA copy — be specific about the action

---

## Hero Section

The Campaign Brief frame creates a distinctive first impression that ties back to the craft of marketing. Think of the "To / From / Re" fields as a subject line — punchy, specific, scannable.

Good: "Social media & email strategy that actually converts"
Bad: "Helping brands achieve their digital marketing goals"

---

## Bio / About Page

Write in first person. Cover:
1. How you got into this field
2. What you're uniquely good at (specifics)
3. What kind of work you want more of

Match the founder bio energy from wellcopy/about — casual, direct, personal. No corporate voice.

---

## CTA Copy

- Primary: "Let's Talk" / "Start a conversation" / "See the Work"
- Secondary: "All Work →" / "Read more →"
- Avoid: "Get in touch", "Learn more", "Schedule a consultation"

---

## Numbers & Stats

Always use real numbers where possible. Estimates are fine but be consistent.
- "10+ years" not "over a decade"
- "50+ brands" not "many brands"
- "10M+ accounts reached" — include the + to signal it's a floor, not a ceiling
